ABOUT ME

My name is Maurice Blake and my major is Game Design. In the future I would like to work in the video gaming industry as a game designer. I'm a fan of many different gaming genres such as fighting sports, simulation, puzzle, real time strategy and adventure games. I am a driven and self-motived individual who always puts out a 100 percent. I was born on the island of Jamaica the younger of one sibling and the older of two. I'm very athletic and spend my free time playing soccer. When not playing soccer I'm playing video games. I decided I wanted to be a game designer because of the love I have for being creative and I enjoy troubleshooting technical problems. In quoting one of the most influential designers of our time (Steve Jobs)

Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.
I believe in this statement that being a designer comes down to how well your designs work and use this as an inspiration to achieve the best designs possible.

ABOUT PERSONA

NiashaNanaAnthony is a well renowned master chef of Fine arts and Cuisines, who works and lives in the country of Jamaica. She's the master chef of the prestigious 5 star Sandals hotel located in Negril, Jamaica. A mother of 2 beautiful girls and married to Managing Director of Global Technology Marquis Anthony. Niasha was born September 11 th 1978 in Kingston Jamaica. She was raised by her single mother Geneva Dunkley, and the only sister of five older brothers. From an early age Niasha has always dreamt of being a chef. Her love for the kitchen came at a tender age of 11. Being the youngest of her siblings and the only girl she normally found herself in the kitchen around her mother. Niasha explained that spending time in the kitchen with her mother helped to nurture the bound she shares with her and love she has for creating exquisite cuisines.

Niasha was recognized while attending Bridgeport High School for her passion and creative approach to cooking by winning the Helen Morris award 3 years in a row for young aspiring chefs. These achievements led to a full scholarship to attend the University of the West Indies where she attained a Masters degree in Culinary Arts. During the last 5 years she has constituently dominated the culinary scene in the tourism industry creating a name for herself by setting a golden standard in tantalizing taste buds of people from all over the world. As the current holder of the prestigious Jackie Bellard Award given to the most influential, successful and creative chefs in the tourism industry, which she has won the last 4 years running. Niasha says she is now ready to take her career to the next level by creating this website to showcase her work and achievements to the world. This would help by putting her on the world stage of master chefs. She is also working on setting up a video streaming service to showcase her skills in the kitchen, as well as to share her recipes to those who have a passion for cooking. Her cook book titled Nana's Caribbean Delights is also in the works and schedule to be released in the next few months.

Reflection: The Beginning of the Semester

This semester starts with Web Design being a new topic for me and having never did any form of coding or attempt to make a website, I feel my weakest skill is not being familiar to the programs and concepts associated with web design. Additionally I look forward to learning how to use CSS, XHTML, Dreamweaver, Flash and being able to fully utilizing them effectively throughout the course and future endeavors. I found that grasping the use of HTML tags and being able to incorporate them to be a slight challenge such as using subscripts (CO2 and H2O). As a game design major I believe being able to make websites effectively and attractively will help strengthen my career. I plan on improving on my lack of knowledge by applying myself to course material and assignments presented to make full use of the learning opportunities it provides. My strongest skill would definitely be my ability to be driven and my willingness to apply myself to any given task.

Reflection: The Middle of the Semester

At the midpoint of this semester I see where I'm now more familiar with the basic web concepts (CSS, HTML5). I'm now more comfortable with using the Adobe Suite programs such as Dreamweaver. Throughout the classes so far I've discovered that web design can be tedious and require you to be willing to apply yourself. I'm now stronger in my approach towards my assignments, before I was a bit scared to work on my own with new material. Development is needed in utilizing design tools such as Photoshop and Illustrator more efficiently. I tend to struggle with using them to their full potential. My progress throughout this semester so far can be noted in my Building Blocks. I believe they represent the journey and understanding I've gain on the course materials up to this point.

Reflection: The End of the Semester

I think my most significant accomplishment this semester is being able to actually create a working webiste using HTML and CSS. I beleive this is a major step for me because at the start of the semester I was clueless to web design and to be able to endure it's tedius learning curve. Yes, I'm now closer to becoming a well-rounded game designer. Now that I'm able to create websites using the basics I've learned, I can now reach an expanded gaming market. My final project is a major achievement for me as I didnt understand alot of CSS and scripts until my last few iterations of my website.
